# Product Philosophy

## What TPS Is

The Trading Product Specification (TPS) is an open, versioned, AI-native design and product standard for professional trading platforms.

It defines how trading products should look, behave, communicate, and evolve — providing a shared language for designers, engineers, and product managers building in the trading domain.

---

## What TPS Is Not

| TPS Is | TPS Is Not |
|--------|------------|
| A specification standard | A UI component library |
| A normative reference | A prescriptive framework |
| Domain-specific | General-purpose |
| Open and versioned | Proprietary or locked |
| AI-native | AI-dependent |

TPS does not ship production-ready components. It defines what compliant components must do, look like, and handle.

## Target Audience

### Primary
- **Product Managers** building trading platforms
- **UX/UI Designers** creating trading interfaces
- **Frontend Engineers** implementing trading components
- **Design System Teams** at financial institutions

### Secondary
- **Regulators** seeking interface compliance references
- **Researchers** studying financial UX
- **Educators** teaching fintech product design

---

## Product Principles

### Open by Default
All specifications are publicly available, versioned, and free to use. Commercial use is permitted under the project license.

### Community Governed
Major specification changes require community review. No single organization controls the roadmap.

### Incrementally Adoptable
Teams can adopt TPS one specification at a time. Full adoption is not required to derive value.

### Backward Compatible
Breaking changes follow a formal deprecation process. Stability is a product commitment.

---

## Success Metrics

TPS measures success by:

1. **Adoption** — Number of products referencing TPS specifications
2. **Contribution** — Volume and quality of community contributions
3. **Coverage** — Percentage of trading UI surface area specified
4. **Compliance tooling** — Availability of automated compliance checking
